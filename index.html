About Me

I'm a preceptor at Harvard University, mostly doing software development, devops, system administration and a little bit of teaching for CS50, Harvard's introduction to computer science and programming, and a couple of other courses.

I graduated from Helwan University with a bachelor's degree in accounting in 2017, but I have always had a passion for computers. I started studying computer science and programming using online resources in 2014, made my first contribution to an open source project in July 2015, and officially joined CS50's team at Harvard in June 2017.

Projects

I help design, develop, and maintain the CS50 IDE, a customized version of Cloud9, a cloud-based IDE that is now one of Amazon Web Services, to better fit students with little or no prior computer science or programming experience by means of creating and maintaining a set of plugins that simplify the look and feel of the IDE as well as installing and configuring the software that is needed for the course.

I also develop and maintain an offline version of the CS50 IDE that uses Docker, a popular containerization technology. Recently, I have been focusing on switching our users to a customized version of AWS Cloud9 which uses a new version of Cloud9 SDK and would be hosted on Amazon infrastructure that we manage.
